# Day 1 LinkedIn post

Day 1 of 45: I'm becoming a proper roboticist, in public.

Here's my confession: I'm president of Yale's undergraduate robotics club, and I've never built a robot end to end. I have the pieces (software, CAD, EE, controls classes). What I've never built is the connective tissue: the loop every robot runs. Sense the world, estimate where you are, plan a path, act on it.

So for the next 45 days I'm building that tissue one day at a time. One lesson a day, one thing that runs a day, every bug logged publicly. It all converges on a single flagship demo: an autonomous mobile manipulator. A robot that maps a building it has never seen, drives itself around it, then picks up an object and delivers it. Think hospital fetch-and-deliver, entirely in simulation.

The rules, every day with no exceptions:

• watch something visual first (intuition before formalism)
• build something that actually runs
• log what broke, why, and the fix

Today was the foundation: the mental model of what a robot even is as a system, plus my first two ROS 2 programs talking to each other (ROS 2 is the open-source nervous system most modern robots run on, and it clicked fast: it's a message bus wearing a lab coat).

Everything is open source and free to steal, notebooks, curriculum, and all: https://github.com/filippo-fonseca/robotics-from-scratch
